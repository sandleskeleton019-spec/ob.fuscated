Thanh Hoa City

Discover historic citadels, sun-drenched coastlines, and a rich culinary tradition in Central Vietnam.

Top Attractions

Sam Son Beach

Located just 16km from the city center, this iconic 9km coastline is famous for its golden sand, gentle waves, and historic Truong Le Mountain shrines.

Ham Rong Bridge

Spanning the Ma River, this landmark stands right inside the city bounds as an indomitable architectural icon of history and resilience.

Citadel of the Ho Dynasty

A recognized UNESCO World Heritage site constructed from enormous, precisely carved stone blocks without the use of mortar.

Pu Luong Nature Reserve

A spectacular highland getaway featuring sweeping green rice terraces, traditional stilt villages, and pristine jungle trekking routes.

Local Culinary Specialties

The unique geography of Thanh Hoa yields incredible combinations of sweet, tangy, and savory street delicacies.

Specialty Description Flavor Profile
Nem Chua Thanh Hoa's signature minced pork roll fermented naturally in tight banana leaves. Tangy, sharp, and firmly chewy.
Chả Tôm Freshly caught ground shrimp patties wrapped inside crisp rice sheets, grilled directly over charcoal. Crispy outer layer with a smoky, sweet interior.
Bánh Răng Bừa A delicate steamed rice flour wrap packed with savory minced pork and wood-ear mushrooms. Soft, silky, and deeply aromatic.
Chè Lam Phú Quảng Traditional cake confection blocks made using roasted sticky rice flour, molasses, and fresh ginger. Densely chewy and sweet with a ginger kick.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

April to August is ideal for enjoying the coastal waters of Sam Son. Go between September and November for hiking in Pu Luong.

Getting Around

Metered taxis and ride-hailing apps operate smoothly within the city. Renting a motorbike is great for scenic highway cruising.

Souvenir Shopping

Always pick up packs of Nem Chua to take home. Most local shops bundle them in batches of 10 or 20 rolls.
